What is required to travel?

A passport! Passports are required for any trip outside of the U.S. (not including Hawaii, Puerto Rico, and the U.S. Virgin Islands). If you are headed to an all-inclusive destination a passport will be required. Any trips within the U.S. will require a legal form of identification, which may include a U.S. driver's license. Other countries may require other documents like a customs form or travel Visa. In order to find these country specific requirements, we encourage clients to visit Travel.State.Gov.

When should I book my trip?

We suggest trips be booked at least six months prior to departure! There is typically last minute availability; however, it might not be your ideal vacation or price point. We suggest giving yourself plenty of time to guarantee the best price. It will also ensure that there are plenty of available rooms at resorts/hotels and seats on the flight. Air is often available within nine months of departure. 

How do I pay for my trip?

In most cases outside of 45 days from departure a deposit will be required to lock in your spot and the given total price. The final payment date will be set between 50 days for all-inclusive destination or 75-120 days before cruises and other guided tours. No payment plan is required, and we can put in payments anytime they are requested! Once final payment time rolls around we will need the remaining balance paid so your trip does not cancel out!

Do I need Travel Insurace?

Travel insurance is highly encouraged! In the past few years we have seen Maui wildfires, hurricanes off the coast, and of course a pandemic. All of these unforeseen events lead to trip delays and cancellations. Luckily, many of our clients had travel protection to re-coop any losses and process refunds. Many of our all-inclusive or air and hotel packages offer a cancel for any reason protection. Travel protection often covers sickness, injury or death of a loved one; quarantine; jury duty/sub poena; financial default. They may also include coverage for trip delay, missed connection, lost or delayed baggage, medical, dental, and evacuation.
